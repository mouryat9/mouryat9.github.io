About Me

I am a PhD student in Computer Science at Kennesaw State University (GPA: 4.0/4.0). My dissertation focuses on Multimodal AI systems for interactive learning — spanning LLM fine-tuning, RAG, multi-agent architectures, and LLM-as-a-Judge evaluation.

My research lies at the intersection of Large Language Models, Retrieval-Augmented Generation, and Agentic AI. I develop adaptive, autonomous systems for interactive and personalized applications, with a focus on education and document understanding.

I have published 9 peer-reviewed papers across top AI venues including AIED, SIGCSE, ASEE, and FIE, and I serve as a reviewer for ICML. I have completed two research internships at HP Inc., delivering production-grade ML systems for edge hardware, including on-device PII detection and LLM-based document extraction.

Prior to my PhD, I received my M.S. in Computer Science from St. Cloud State University and worked as a Senior Software Developer at UST, building intelligent document understanding systems using NLP and deep learning.

I am always open to collaborations and discussions. Feel free to reach out via email!
