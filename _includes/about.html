About

I am a PhD student in Computer Science at Kennesaw State University (GPA: 4.0/4.0). My dissertation investigates Multimodal AI for interactive learning, building systems that process lecture videos (audio, visual, OCR), generate Bloom's Taxonomy-aligned assessments via cognitive scaffolding, and deliver adaptive tutoring through Bloom's-aware RAG.

My broader research spans LLMs, Retrieval-Augmented Generation, multi-agent architectures, and AI evaluation. I am building CogBench, the first benchmark for evaluating cognitive-level control in LLM question generation (27K+ annotated questions), and AgentPDPR, an agentic recommender that helps educators discover evidence-based teaching strategies through multi-step reasoning.

I have published 9 peer-reviewed papers at AIED, SIGCSE, ASEE, and FIE, serve as a reviewer for ICML, and completed two research internships at HP Inc. deploying on-device AI for edge hardware. Prior to my PhD, I received my M.S. from St. Cloud State University and worked at UST building intelligent document understanding systems.
